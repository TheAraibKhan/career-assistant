How This Works

A map of where you are — not a list of failures

This tool compares your current skills against what a target role typically requires. The result isn't a verdict — it's a map. You'll see what you already have, what's partially covered, and what's worth building next.

The goal isn't to fill every gap before you apply. It's to understand which gaps matter most for your direction — and which ones can wait.

Why This Stage Matters

You don't need to be perfect — you need to be aligned

Most students try to close every gap before they feel ready. That's not how growth works. Understanding which gaps actually block your path — versus which are nice-to-haves — lets you spend your learning time on things that genuinely move you forward.

The skill map this tool produces isn't a checklist to complete. It's a reference point to revisit as you grow. As you build new skills, run it again and watch the picture change.

How to Use This

Work with what you have, build toward what's next

  • Add the role's exact language — If the job says "cross-functional collaboration," use that phrase, not "team communication"
  • Be honest about partial matches — If you've used Kubernetes lightly, mention it with context rather than omitting it
  • Lead with the strongest matches — Put your most relevant experience near the top of each section
  • Prioritize must-haves over nice-to-haves — Job descriptions list requirements in rough order of importance
