What's the Problem?

Job titles are deceiving. A "Software Engineer" at one company needs different skills than at another. You might have strong technical skills but lack what this specific role needs, and vice versa.

Most candidates don't strategically align their resume with the role they're targeting. This gap shows as "not quite right fit" in recruiter feedback‚Äîeven when you're qualified.

Why This Matters

Recruiters screen resumes against role requirements. If you're missing 2-3 key skills, your resume gets filtered. You might be capable of learning them, but the initial screen doesn't account for potential.

Your career trajectory depends on knowing: What do I have? What does this role need? What's the fastest path to close the gap?

How to Close the Gap

  • Identify your target role - Be specific (not just "manager," but which type)
  • Extract required skills - Review job descriptions for patterns
  • Highlight transferable skills - Show how existing experience translates
  • Plan skill development - Online courses, projects, or on-the-job training
  • Quantify your progress - Show improvement through metrics and examples
  • Reframe your narrative - Explain the gap as a growth trajectory, not a weakness

Smart Next Steps

Instead of being discouraged by gaps, use them as a roadmap. The fastest path to your target role is filling these specific, identified gaps‚Äînot becoming an all-around generalist.
