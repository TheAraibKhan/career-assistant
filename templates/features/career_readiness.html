Score Tiers & What They Mean

  • Early Stage (0–40)

    Your resume needs significant work. Likely being rejected in early screens. Focus on ATS compatibility and content basics.

  • Building (41–60)

    You're on the right track but have clear gaps. Recruiters might call, but you're missing opportunities. Prioritize missing skills and bullet strength.

  • Solid (61–75)

    Your resume is doing its job. You'll land interviews. Small improvements can push you higher. Focus on relevance to specific roles.

  • Well-Developed (76+)

    Your resume is competitive. You're likely to get interviews when you apply to aligned roles. Continue improving to stand out from other strong candidates.

This isn't a checklist. Each stage builds on the previous one. Submit a new version whenever you've made meaningful changes — the trend will tell its own story.

Ongoing Reflection

  • Step 1: Upload your current resume and get a baseline score. Don't overthink it.
  • Step 2: Make one focused improvement. Structure, alignment, or a single bullet rewrite.
  • Step 3: Upload the updated version. See if the score moved. Understand what changed.
  • Step 4: Repeat over time. Your history stays in your account. The trend across versions is your actual story.

Your First Snapshot

Each time you upload a new version of your resume, we add it to your timeline. Over time, you'll have a record of how your resume has changed — and a clear sense of where you've grown.

Most people find that consistent, small improvements build into significant progress over a few months. The score is a reference — not the destination.
