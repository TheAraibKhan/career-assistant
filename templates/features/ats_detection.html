What's the Problem?

ATS systems are designed to parse resumes and extract key information. They're not sophisticated‚Äîthey stumble on formatting, images, tables, and unusual fonts. If the ATS can't read your resume properly, it never reaches a human recruiter, no matter how qualified you are.

Common ATS Killers:

  • Tables & complex layouts - ATS reads text sequentially and gets confused
  • Images, graphics, or icons - Most ATS can't interpret visual elements
  • Headers, footers, or sidebars - Information in margins often gets lost
  • Unusual fonts - Fancy typefaces fail to parse correctly
  • PDF formatting issues - Improper conversion destroys structure
  • Missing keywords - ATS searches for specific terms; if they're absent, you're invisible

Why This Matters

Imagine this: You're perfectly qualified for a role, but your resume never reaches the hiring manager because the ATS couldn't parse it. A technical failure, not a content one, ends your opportunity. This happens to thousands of candidates every day.

The reality: Your resume must clear the ATS filter before your qualifications even matter. It's a gatekeeper, and many candidates don't realize they've already been filtered out.

How to Fix It

  • Use a clean, simple format - Stick to bullet points and standard margins
  • Remove images and icons - ATS software treats them as blank space
  • Use standard fonts - Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman are safest
  • Avoid tables - Use tabs and spaces instead to organize information
  • Include relevant keywords - Mirror language from the job description
  • Save as .docx or .pdf - Avoid exotic file formats
