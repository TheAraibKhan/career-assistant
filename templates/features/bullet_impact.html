How We Score

What makes a bullet strong — and what doesn't

We break each bullet point into four components that predict whether a recruiter will actually read it or skim past it. Each dimension gets a score from 1 to 10, and you see the total for every line.

Specificity of Results
Does this bullet describe a concrete outcome? "Improved performance" scores low; "reduced load time by 40%" scores high.
Verb Strength
"Led," "built," and "launched" signal ownership. "Helped," "assisted," and "worked on" signal participation. Recruiters notice the difference.
Quantified Impact
Numbers make impact real and memorable. Team size, revenue, timelines, percentages — any measurable signal counts.
ATS Keyword Fit
Does this bullet contain language that matches your target role? Relevance to the job description affects whether the resume passes screening.

How to Improve Your Scores

Practical rewrites that raise the bar

  • Rewrite every bullet that starts with "helped" or "assisted" — You did the work; claim it with a direct verb
  • Add at least one number per role — Even rough estimates ("served 200+ customers") are better than no numbers
  • Use the CAR format — Context, Action, Result keeps bullets focused and scannable
  • Cut bullets scoring below 5 — If you can't strengthen them, remove them; weak bullets dilute your strong ones
