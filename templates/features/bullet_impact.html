The Problem: Weak Bullet Points

"Responsible for managing projects." "Contributed to team success." "Helped with various tasks." If you've written bullets like these, you're not alone‚Äîand you're hurting your chances.

Weak bullets:

  • Don't show clear impact
  • Are vague and generic
  • Don't explain WHY this matters
  • Waste recruiter time
  • Blend in with competitors' resumes

The Bullet Point Impact Formula

Action Verb (delivered, grew, designed, built) + What You Did (the work) + Measurable Result (the impact) + Context (scope/business value)
Example: "Designed and launched mobile app that reduced checkout time by 64% (from 3.2min to 1.1min), increasing conversion rate by 28% and generating $1.2M in additional revenue"

Why This Matters

Your bullets are your main sales pitch. Recruiters scan them to answer: "Did this person achieve results?" Weak bullets force them to infer impact‚Äîand they usually infer the worst.

Strong bullets make your resume undeniable. They show, don't tell. They prove value.

How to Score Higher

  • Start with strong action verbs - "Achieved," "Delivered," "Transformed," not "Helped" or "Assisted"
  • Include at least one metric - Percentage growth, revenue, time saved, customers won
  • Explain the business impact - Why does this result matter?
  • Use the job description - Mirror language where you have matching accomplishments
  • Keep to 1-2 lines - Concise > detailed for resume bullets
  • Remove generic language - "Team player," "self-starter," "detail-oriented" add zero value on a resume
